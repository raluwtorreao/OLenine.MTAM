Albums

EITA, 2025 His newest album marks the artist’s return with a vibrant work deeply connected to its time. The record reinforces the sonic identity that established Lenine, blending Northeastern rhythms, MPB, rock, and electronic experimentation, without giving up poetry and reflection. The songs engage with everyday life, addressing themes such as social transformations, astonishment in the face of the contemporary world, and creativity as a force of resistance.

After 10 years away from production, Lenine presents his latest project EITA as a breath of relief and continuity. The album’s central theme is rooted in his cultural and personal origins, focusing on what he achieved thanks to the environment in which he was raised. In this way, alongside his newest record, Lenine also gave rise to his latest audiovisual production of the same name: “EITA!”. In it, he talks about his origins, the paths he followed in life, and his musical and intimate journey. EITA! is now available.

Carbono, 2015 Presents reflections on the relationship between humanity, nature, and technology. The album addresses environmental impacts, excessive consumption, and imbalances caused by accelerated progress. With electronic sonorities and elements of MPB, the songs propose awareness, responsibility, and reflection on the future of the planet, combining environmental criticism with contemporary musical identity.

Chão, 2011 Is an intimate and sensitive album that celebrates affection as a vital force. The songs speak of the finitude of things, the passing of time, and the fragility of existence, without losing their charm. In every track, life blossoms, along with the desire to go on, to love, and to fall in love with the world, even in the face of loss, reaffirming the beauty of being alive. The album invites attentive listening and an embrace of the present, with delicacy and courage.

Labiata, 2008 Dives into experimental rock while dialoguing with Brazilian coastal culture. The album expresses appreciation for the beach, the sea, and coastal life, while reflecting on uncontrolled urban expansion. The songs address environmental tragedies such as rising sea levels and oil spills, combining ecological criticism, poetry, and intense sonic energy, reaffirming Lenine as an artist attentive to the present and the urgent now.

Acústico MTV, 2006 Presents a remarkable compilation, curiously absent today from many music platforms. The album values the essential, with clean arrangements and the pure sound of the guitar, revealing nuances of the songs. Highlights include “A Ponte,” one of the artist’s most well-known songs, featuring the notable participation of GOG, rapping about the JK Bridge and questioning the social, urban, and political implications of its construction.

Lenine Incité, 2005 Records a special performance filmed in Paris, France, bringing together standout songs from his career. The album emphasizes refined arrangements and the poetic strength of the compositions, conceived to dialogue with foreign audiences. The recording highlights Lenine’s ability to cross borders, taking Brazilian music abroad with identity, inventiveness, and striking contemporary sensitivity on stage.

Falange Canibal, 2003 Develops under a darker tone by reflecting on the brutality of contemporary times. The album observes tragedies produced by humanity itself, without losing the ability to perceive beauty, affection, and resistance. Amid harsh criticism, sentimental songs emerge, supported by arrangements typical of Northeastern music, balancing density and sensitivity, reaffirming faith in humanity even in the face of chaos.

Na Pressão, 1999 Powerfully synthesizes what Lenine seeks in his music: rough, direct rock, sweetly immersed and tinged with maracatu. The album unites impact and delicacy, rhythm and poetry. The lyrics move between the objective, the subjective, and the troubadour-like, affirming a unique identity and valuing Brazilian sovereignty in the face of foreign influence, without rejecting dialogue, but asserting a firm, critical, and deeply rooted authorial voice.

The Day We Will Make Contact, 1997 Emerges from a blend of Northeastern culture and the extraterrestrial aesthetics of science fiction. The album mixes maracatu, regional rhythms, and futuristic sounds, creating a singular universe. Between cosmic imagery and earthly references, passionate and abstract compositions arise, exploring human sentiment, its affections, fears, and desires, projecting love and existence beyond time, space, and the boundaries of reality.

Olho de Peixe, 1993 Created by Lenine alongside Marcos Suzano, this album is marked by a beach-oriented and deeply Northeastern character. The songs evoke the sea, the wind, and the coastal landscape, with a strong presence of berimbau and organic percussion. The record celebrates the infinity of natural beauty, transforming sound into landscape and inviting contemplation, affection, and sensitive listening to the world and its eternal, free, and ancestral rhythms of the Brazilian coast.
